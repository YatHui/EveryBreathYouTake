Air Pollution

Nitrogen dioxide and air particulate matters (pm) are the common indicators to measure air pollution. Two sizes of particulate matters are measured - coarse particles bigger than 10 microns (pm10) and fine particulates smaller than 2.5 microns (pm2.5). Our bodies can filter out the coarser particles, such as by trapping them with the hair in our nose, or we can cough them out. However, fine particulates are so small, that when we breathe them in, they can easily get into our lungs and bloodstreams. This is why they cause respiratory and cardio dieases.

This is also why we use pm2.5 datasets in this data project.
