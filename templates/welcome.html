How air pollution affects our health

The health effects of air pollution are serious – one third of deaths from stroke, lung cancer and heart disease are due to air pollution. Air pollution is hard to escape, no matter how rich an area you live in. It is all around us. Microscopic pollutants in the air can slip past our body’s defences, penetrating deep into our respiratory and circulatory system, damaging our lungs, heart and brain.

Air-pollution

Air pollution is the contamination of air due to the presence of substances in the atmosphere that are harmful to the health of humans and other living beings, or cause damage to the climate or to materials

Health

Our air quality and health project aims to demonstrate the correlation between air quality and diseases/deaths. The data we provide helps us showcase the number of deaths associated with poor air quality and how our lives can improve when we focus on a better environment
